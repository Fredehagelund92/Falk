# Business Glossary

> Define key business terms and concepts that your team uses.
> Keep definitions canonical - each term should have ONE clear definition.

## Customer Lifecycle

**Lead**
- Definition: Contact with valid email, no account created
- Example: Someone who filled out a contact form

**Prospect**
- Definition: Account created, no purchase yet
- Example: Signed up for trial or freemium account

**Customer**
- Definition: Completed at least one purchase
- Example: Paid for subscription or product

**Active Customer**
- Definition: Customer with activity in last 90 days OR active subscription
- Example: Made a purchase or logged in recently

**Churned Customer**
- Definition: No activity for 180+ days AND no active subscription
- Example: Account dormant for 6+ months

## Time Periods

**MTD (Month-to-Date)**
- From the 1st of current month to today

**QTD (Quarter-to-Date)**
- From the start of current quarter to today

**YTD (Year-to-Date)**
- From January 1st to today

**Trailing 30/60/90 Days**
- Rolling window, not calendar-based
- Example: "Last 30 days" = today minus 29 days through today

## Revenue Terms

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)**
- Normalized monthly value of all active subscriptions
- Excludes one-time fees

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**
- MRR × 12
- Used for B2B SaaS reporting

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**
- Total revenue / number of active customers
- Calculated monthly or annually

**LTV (Lifetime Value)**
- Predicted total revenue from a customer over their lifetime
- Formula: ARPU × (1 / churn_rate)

## Product Terms

**Feature**
- A distinct capability or function in the product
- Example: "Export to CSV", "Dashboard sharing"

**Module**
- A collection of related features
- Example: "Analytics Module", "Reporting Module"

**Plan / Tier**
- A pricing/packaging level
- Example: Starter, Professional, Enterprise

## Marketing Terms

**Campaign**
- A coordinated marketing effort with specific goals
- Has: start date, end date, budget, channels

**Channel**
- Source of traffic or leads
- Examples: Organic, Paid Search, Email, Social, Direct

**Conversion**
- When a user completes a desired action
- Examples: signup, purchase, trial start

**Attribution**
- Which marketing touchpoint gets credit for a conversion
- Default: Last-touch attribution (most recent source)

## How to Use This Glossary

1. **Keep it updated**: Add new terms as your business evolves
2. **Be specific**: Include formulas, examples, edge cases
3. **One definition per term**: Avoid multiple definitions for the same concept
4. **Reference in queries**: The agent can use these when analyzing data
